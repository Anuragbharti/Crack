Motivation and Objective

Cracks are of major concern for the safety, durability and serviceability of structure. The reason is that when cracks are developed and propagate that tend to cause the reduction in effective loading area which brings about the increase of stress and subsequently failure of the concrete or other structures. Cracks create access to harmful and corrosive Chemicals to penetrate Into The structures which consequently damage their integrity as well as aesthetics.

Detection of cracks is a crucial task in monitoring structural health and ensuring structural safety. The manual process of crack detection is painstakingly time consuming and suffers from subjective judgements of the inspector. This project establishes an intelligent model based on ML technique for automatic recognition and analysis. The algorithm can successfully detect the crack defects in digital images. On the basis of detected cracks, it can be identified that which cracks are really harmful and should be repaired first. Therefore constructed model can be a useful tool for building management agencies and construction engineers in the task of structural maintenance.

Cracks Formation

Cracks arise in concrete due to various reasons.Cracks caused before hardening are due to constructional movement, settlement shrinkage, and setting shrinkage. Cracks caused after hardening are due to chemical reactions, physical movement, thermal changes, stress concentrations, structural design, and accidents.

What leads up to a crack? Consider a concrete bar of some reference length ,in some reference condition of moisture and temperature, and unstressed.If the bar is dried or cooled without restraint, it will simply contract and no stress will be developed. If, however, the ends are restrained during drying or cooling to maintain the original length, the bar develops tensile stress just as if it had been allowed to contract freely and then had been pulled back to its original length. As time goes on the stress is gradually relieved in part by creep, or plastic flow or other changes. At any time, whether in fresh or hardened concrete, if the net tensile stress builds up to the tensile strength at that age, the concrete will crack and the stress will be relieved.
